M4 Macbook Pro Review

Overview

The M4 MacBook Pro is a high-performance laptop designed for smooth everyday use and demanding creative work. It offers fast, responsive performance and seamless multitasking, making it ideal for students, professionals, and creators. Its sleek design and long battery life ensure it’s easy to carry and use on the go. Overall, it’s a powerful yet portable option for those who need both efficiency and reliability.

Key Specifications

  • Processor: Apple M4 chip (10‑core CPU)
  • Graphics: Integrated 10‑core GPU
  • Display: 14" Liquid Retina XDR (3024×1964), 120 Hz ProMotion
  • Memory: 16 GB unified (configurable up to 32 GB)
  • Storage: 512 GB SSD (configurable up to 2 TB)
  • OS: macOS
  • Connectivity: Wi‑Fi 6E, Bluetooth 5.3, Thunderbolt 4 / USB‑C, HDMI, MagSafe 3
  • Battery: 72.4 Wh lithium‑polymer battery (up to ~24 hours video)
  • Weight: 1.6 kg

Performance

The Apple M4 chip with its 10‑core CPU and 10‑core GPU delivers smooth performance for creative work, multitasking, and everyday tasks. The MacBook handles apps like Adobe Photoshop, Final Cut Pro, and Logic Pro seamlessly, and it can even run light gaming and AI-assisted workflows efficiently.

Display & Visuals

The 14" Liquid Retina XDR display with ProMotion delivers exceptionally smooth scrolling and animations thanks to its 120 Hz refresh rate. Its high brightness, wide color gamut, and HDR support make visuals vibrant and detailed, ideal for creative work, media consumption, and polished everyday use.

Upgrade Potential

The M4 MacBook Pro has unified memory and SSD storage that aren’t user-upgradable, so performance and capacity must be chosen at purchase, with options up to 32 GB RAM and 2 TB SSD.

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Sleek and portable design, high-performance M4 chip, Liquid Retina XDR display with ProMotion, long battery life, excellent for creative work and multitasking.
  • Cons: Memory and storage not user-upgradeable, limited to light gaming, higher purchase cost compared to similar-sized laptops.
